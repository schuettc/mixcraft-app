# Genre Relationships & Playlist Bridges

A reference for understanding how genres connect and how to build smooth transitions
between them in playlists. Use this when constructing playlists that span multiple genres
or when a user's taste crosses genre boundaries.

## Genre Families

### Rock Family Tree

```
Classic Rock → Hard Rock → Metal
     ↓              ↓
Blues Rock    Punk → Post-Punk → Goth
     ↓              ↓
Southern Rock  Pop Punk → Emo → Midwest Emo
                    ↓
              Indie Rock → Math Rock
                    ↓
              Shoegaze → Dream Pop → Ambient
```

**Bridge artists** (span multiple sub-genres):
- Radiohead: alt-rock → electronic → art rock
- The National: indie rock → post-punk revival → chamber pop
- Wilco: alt-country → indie rock → experimental
- Deerhunter: indie rock → shoegaze → ambient
- Queens of the Stone Age: stoner rock → alt-rock → desert rock

### Electronic Family Tree

```
House → Deep House → Lo-fi House
  ↓
Techno → Minimal Techno → Ambient Techno
  ↓
IDM → Glitch → Experimental Electronic
  ↓
Synth-pop → New Wave → Darkwave
  ↓
Trip-hop → Downtempo → Chillout
```

**Bridge artists:**
- Four Tet: folktronica → house → ambient
- Caribou: psychedelic → electronic → dance
- LCD Soundsystem: dance-punk → electronic → post-punk
- Bonobo: trip-hop → downtempo → dance
- Jamie xx: UK garage → house → ambient

### Folk/Americana Family

```
Traditional Folk → Contemporary Folk → Anti-Folk
       ↓                    ↓
Bluegrass → Newgrass   Singer-Songwriter → Indie Folk
       ↓                    ↓
Country → Alt-Country  Folk Rock → Folk Punk
       ↓
Outlaw Country → Americana
```

**Bridge artists:**
- Sturgill Simpson: outlaw country → psychedelic rock → bluegrass
- Fleet Foxes: indie folk → baroque pop → progressive
- Bon Iver: indie folk → electronic → experimental
- The Avett Brothers: folk punk → americana → pop
- Iron & Wine: folk → ambient folk → indie rock

### Hip-Hop/R&B Family

```
Boom Bap → Conscious Hip-Hop → Jazz Rap
    ↓              ↓
Trap → Drill   Alternative Hip-Hop → Abstract Hip-Hop
    ↓              ↓
Cloud Rap    Neo-Soul → Alternative R&B → PBR&B
```

**Bridge artists:**
- Tyler, the Creator: hip-hop → neo-soul → jazz
- Frank Ocean: R&B → indie → electronic
- Anderson .Paak: hip-hop → funk → soul

## Genre Bridging Strategies

### By Shared Characteristics

When transitioning between genres, find songs that share at least one quality:

| From | To | Bridge via |
|------|----|-----------|
| Indie Rock | Electronic | Indie electronic (MGMT, Tame Impala) |
| Hip-Hop | Jazz | Jazz rap (A Tribe Called Quest, Nujabes) |
| Folk | Rock | Folk rock (The Lumineers, Mumford era) |
| R&B | Electronic | Alternative R&B (FKA twigs, Kelela) |
| Punk | Electronic | Dance-punk (LCD Soundsystem, !!!) |
| Country | Rock | Southern rock, alt-country (Drive-By Truckers) |
| Classical | Electronic | Neoclassical (Nils Frahm, Max Richter) |
| Metal | Electronic | Industrial (Nine Inch Nails, Ministry) |

### By Era

Songs from the same era share production aesthetics even across genres:

- **Late 70s**: Punk, disco, and new wave share the same rawness and energy
- **Mid 80s**: Synth-pop, new wave, and post-punk all share synthesizer aesthetics
- **Early 90s**: Grunge, shoegaze, and trip-hop share a lo-fi, moody quality
- **Late 90s**: Electronica, big beat, and alt-rock share maximalist production
- **Mid 2000s**: Indie rock, blog house, and folktronica share DIY warmth
- **2010s**: Chillwave, witch house, and cloud rap share hazy, reverb-heavy production
- **2020s**: Hyperpop, bedroom pop, and PC Music share digital maximalism

### By Tempo/Energy

Group songs by BPM range for smooth energy flow:

| Energy | BPM Range | Genres that fit |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Ambient/Chill | 60-80 | Ambient, lo-fi, downtempo, ballads |
| Laid-back | 80-100 | Trip-hop, R&B, reggae, folk |
| Mid-tempo | 100-120 | Indie rock, alt-pop, hip-hop |
| Upbeat | 120-135 | Pop, house, funk, dance-rock |
| High energy | 135-150 | Techno, drum & bass, punk |
| Intense | 150+ | Hardcore, gabber, speed metal |

## Mood-to-Genre Mapping

When users describe a mood rather than a genre:

| Mood | Primary genres | Discovery angles |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| "Focus/coding" | Post-rock, ambient electronic, lo-fi | Minimalist classical, drone |

## Sub-Genre Nuances

Understanding sub-genre distinctions prevents mismatches:

### "Indie" Is Not a Genre

"Indie" means different things to different people:
- **Indie rock** (Pavement, Built to Spill) — guitar-forward, rough edges
- **Indie pop** (Belle and Sebastian, Camera Obscura) — melodic, jangly
- **Indie folk** (Iron & Wine, Sufjan Stevens) — acoustic, intimate
- **Indie electronic** (Caribou, Washed Out) — synthesizers, reverb

When a user says "I like indie," check their library to understand which flavor.

### "Alternative" Is Even Vaguer

Could mean anything from Depeche Mode to Billie Eilish. Apple Music's "Alternative"
genre tag is broad. Cross-reference with the actual sound, not just the label.

### Country Subdivisions Matter

Many people who "don't like country" actually love:
- **Outlaw country** (Waylon Jennings, Willie Nelson)
- **Alt-country** (Uncle Tupelo, Wilco's early stuff)
- **Americana** (Jason Isbell, Brandi Carlile)
- **Country-folk** (Townes Van Zandt, Blaze Foley)

What they usually dislike is **mainstream pop-country** (stadium country, bro-country).
Knowing this distinction helps a lot.
